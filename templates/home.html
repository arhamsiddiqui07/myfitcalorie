Nutrition Guide

What you need to know about food, calories & fitness

🎯
Calories In, Calories Out
Weight management is driven by one principle: energy balance. Eat fewer calories than your body uses, and you lose weight. Eat more, and you gain. But calories are not the whole story — the quality and source of what you eat also affects how your body feels and performs.
📏
Losing Weight the Right Way
Around 0.45kg lost per week is a sustainable, healthy target. Cutting 500 calories daily gets you there. Going beyond 1000 kcal deficit daily is not advised — it can cause fatigue and muscle loss. Weigh yourself weekly for a clearer trend.
📱
Track What You Eat
Most people underestimate their food intake by 20 to 40 percent. Even a few weeks of tracking builds an intuitive sense of portion sizes — so eventually, you can eat well without measuring every bite.
🥗
Food Quality Still Matters
Technically you can lose weight eating anything in a deficit. But what you eat shapes how you feel. Whole foods keep you fuller longer and provide nutrients that processed snacks cannot.
🍽️
Portion Size Is Everything
Restaurant servings can be two to three times larger than what your body needs. Eating from a smaller plate and slowing down while eating are habits that consistently reduce intake without feeling like dieting.
🔁
Find What Works for You
There is no single diet that works for everyone. The most effective approach is whichever one you can stick to consistently — whether that means meal prepping, intermittent fasting, or simply eating smaller portions.

Exercise Puts Food in Perspective

Once you realise that a 220-calorie snack requires roughly 25 minutes of running to burn off, the choice becomes much easier. Tracking both food and exercise together creates a feedback loop that naturally steers you toward smarter choices.
