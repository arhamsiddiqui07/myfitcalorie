Nutrition Guide

What you need to know about food, calories & fitness

🎯
Calories In, Calories Out
Weight management is driven by one principle: energy balance. Eat fewer calories than your body uses, and you lose weight. Eat more, and you gain. The quality and source of food also affects how your body feels and performs.
📏
Losing Weight Safely
Around 0.45kg lost per week is a safe, sustainable target. A 500 kcal daily deficit gets you there without extreme restriction. Going beyond 1000 kcal deficit daily risks fatigue and muscle loss.
🥩
Protein is King
Protein keeps you full, preserves muscle during fat loss, and burns more calories to digest. Whether cutting or bulking, hit your protein target daily. Aim for 1.6–2g per kg of bodyweight.
🍚
Carbs & Fats: Balance
Carbs fuel workouts. Fats support hormones. Neither should be eliminated — balance is everything. Choose rice, oats, sweet potato for carbs. Avocado, nuts, olive oil for fats.
🍽️
Portion Control
Restaurant servings can be 2-3x larger than needed. Using a smaller plate, slowing down while eating, and measuring portions for a few weeks builds lasting habits without feeling like dieting.
💧
Hydration Matters
Drinking enough water boosts metabolism by up to 30% for 90 minutes after drinking. Dehydration slows fat burning and makes you feel hungry. Aim for 2.5–3 liters daily.

Exercise Puts Food in Perspective

Once you realise that a 220-calorie snack requires roughly 25 minutes of running to burn off, the choice becomes much easier. Tracking food and exercise together creates a feedback loop that steers you toward smarter choices naturally.
