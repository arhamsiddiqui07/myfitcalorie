Nutrition Guide

What you need to know about food, calories & fitness

🎯
Calories In, Calories Out
Weight management is driven by one principle: energy balance. Eat fewer calories than your body uses and you lose weight. Eat more, and you gain. The quality of food also affects how your body performs long term.
📏
Losing Weight Safely
Around 0.45kg lost per week is a safe target. A 500 kcal daily deficit gets you there. Going beyond 1000 kcal deficit risks fatigue and muscle loss. Weigh yourself weekly for a clearer trend.
🥩
Protein is King
Protein keeps you full, preserves muscle during fat loss, and burns more calories to digest. Whether cutting or bulking, hit your protein target daily. Aim for 1.6–2g per kg of bodyweight.
🍚
Carbs & Fats: Balance
Carbs fuel workouts. Fats support hormones. Neither should be eliminated — balance is everything. Choose rice, oats, sweet potato for carbs. Avocado, nuts, olive oil for fats.

Exercise Puts Food in Perspective

Once you realise a 220-calorie snack requires ~25 minutes of running, the choice becomes easier. Tracking food and exercise together creates a feedback loop that steers you toward smarter choices.

😴
Sleep Affects Fat Loss
Bad sleep raises hunger hormones, leading to 300–500 extra calories consumed the next day. Getting 7–9 hours nightly is one of the most underrated fat-loss tools available.
🏋️
Muscle Burns Fat
Each kg of muscle burns ~13 extra kcal/day at rest. 5kg of added muscle means 2,000+ extra calories burned monthly without extra effort. Strength training upgrades your metabolism permanently.
