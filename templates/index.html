Nutrition Guide

What you need to know about food, calories & fitness

🎯
Calories In, Calories Out
Weight management is driven by one principle: energy balance. Eat fewer calories than your body uses, and you lose weight. Eat more, and you gain. But calories are not the whole story — the quality and source of what you eat also affects how your body feels, performs, and how easily you stick to your plan long term.
📏
Losing Weight the Right Way
Around 0.45kg lost per week is a sustainable, healthy target. Cutting 500 calories daily gets you there without extreme restriction. Going beyond a 1000 kcal daily deficit is not advised — it can cause fatigue, muscle loss, and nutritional deficiencies. Always consult a doctor if you're aiming for faster results. Weigh yourself weekly for a clearer trend.
📱
Track What You Eat
Most people underestimate their food intake by 20 to 40 percent. Calorie tracking apps make it easy to log meals, exercise, and progress in one place. Even a few weeks of tracking builds an intuitive sense of portion sizes and calorie content — so eventually, you can eat well without obsessively measuring every bite.
🥗
Food Quality Still Matters
Technically you can lose weight eating anything — as long as you stay in a deficit. But what you eat shapes how you feel. Whole foods like vegetables, lean proteins, fruits, and grains keep you fuller for longer and provide nutrients that processed snacks cannot. Better food choices mean fewer cravings and a more sustainable routine.

Exercise Puts Food in Perspective

Once you realise that a 220-calorie snack requires roughly 25 minutes of running to burn off, the choice becomes much easier. Tracking both food and exercise together — even loosely — creates a feedback loop that naturally steers you toward smarter choices without relying purely on willpower.

🍽️
Portion Size Is Everything
Many people overeat simply because they've normalised oversized portions. Restaurant servings can be two to three times larger than what the body actually needs. Eating from a smaller plate, measuring portions for a few weeks, and slowing down while eating are small habits that consistently reduce intake without feeling like dieting.
🔁
Find What Works for You
There is no single diet that works for everyone. Calorie counting is one tool — not the only one. The most effective approach is whichever one you can stick to consistently. Whether that means meal prepping, intermittent fasting, or simply eating smaller portions, the best plan is the one that fits your actual lifestyle without feeling like punishment.
